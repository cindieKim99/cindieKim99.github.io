    Introduction to AAVE¶

    Aave is a decentralized cryptocurrency platform that facilitates borrowing and lending of digital assets. Its operational framework revolves around the utilization of smart contracts, which automate various processes such as fund distribution, collateral management, and fee calculation.

    Aave specializes in overcollateralized loans, requiring users to deposit crypto assets worth an amount greater than the value of their intended borrowing. This unique approach offers protection to lenders against losses resulting from loan defaults, as the Aave protocol possesses the capability to liquidate the collateral if its value experiences a significant decline.

    Additionally, Aave provides a native crypto token called AAVE, which serves dual purposes. Firstly, it can be traded on the majority of cryptocurrency exchanges, enabling users to actively engage in the market. Secondly, it can be staked within the Aave platform, entitling participants to earn interest on their holdings. Staking, in this context, refers to the process through which crypto miners receive rewards for validating transactions on a proof-of-stake blockchain, similar to the underlying technology of the Aave protocol.
